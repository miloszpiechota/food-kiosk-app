# Glossary

## Admin Panel
The protected area of the application used by restaurant administrators to view and manage orders.

## Administrator
A user with authorized access to the admin panel for managing restaurant operations in the system.

## Basket
The temporary collection of selected products, meal choices, and personalization details chosen by a customer before checkout. Also commonly called a cart.

## Category
A grouping of products that helps customers browse the menu more easily.

## Checkout
The step in the ordering flow where the customer reviews the order and proceeds to payment.

## Customer
A restaurant guest using the kiosk interface to browse the menu and place an order.

## Kiosk
A self-service terminal-style interface used by customers to order food without staff assistance.

## Localization
The process of presenting the application in supported languages and adapting user-facing text for those languages.

## Locale
A language or regional language setting selected for the kiosk experience, such as English or Polish.

## Menu
The list of available products offered by the restaurant through the kiosk application, including standalone items, meals, and large meals.

## Menu Availability
The rules that determine when a menu is visible or orderable, for example by date, day, or time.

## Menu Category
A category assignment inside a specific menu. The same reusable category may appear in multiple menus with different visibility or scheduling.

## Menu Product
A product assignment inside a specific menu or menu category. The same product may appear in multiple menus with different prices or availability.

## Meal
A configurable product made from one or more selection groups, such as choosing a sandwich, side, and drink.

## Meal Group
A required or optional selection group inside a configurable meal, such as sandwich, side, or drink.

## Modifier
A personalization option that allows a customer to change a selected product, for example by adding or removing ingredients.

## MVP
Minimum Viable Product. The smallest useful version of the application that delivers the core ordering and admin management flow.

## Order
A customer purchase request created through the kiosk system. An order may exist before payment is fully confirmed.

## Order Status
The operational state of an order from the restaurant perspective, such as new, in progress, ready, or completed.

## Payment Status
The state of payment verification, such as pending, paid, failed, cancelled, or awaiting confirmation.

## Product
A purchasable catalog entity. In this project, a product can be a standalone item, a meal, or a large meal.

## Restaurant
The single restaurant that owns the menus, categories, products, and kiosk configuration in the MVP.

## Stripe Checkout
Stripe's hosted payment flow used to handle customer payment in test mode for this project.

## Test Mode
A non-production payment environment used for development and testing without real financial transactions.

## Touch-Friendly UI
An interface designed so users can interact easily through tapping, including clear controls and sufficiently large interactive targets.

## Upsell
A suggestion shown to the customer to encourage adding a complementary product before finalizing basket choices. This is planned as a later feature, not an MVP feature.

## Webhook
A backend endpoint that receives event notifications from another service, such as Stripe payment updates, for verification and processing.
